Toyota Dealer Portal

Introduction

In this student project, my team and I had the opportunity to improve communication and synergy across car dealerships of the same manufacturer. For this project, we chose Toyota, as one of its dealerships was proximally close to our university and provided ample opportunity for user research.

Current dealerships are not only geographically distant, but functionally misaligned in their approaches to business strategy and customer experience. Our team sought to remedy this by designing the Toyota Dealer Portal, a platform that keeps inventory of all the cars in Toyota dealerships per regional area. This platform also enables dealers to communicate with one another and mark the status of a car during sale.

Note: Toyota is not affiliated with this project.

Project details

  • Role: UX designer
  • Date: September 2020
  • Duration: 4 weeks
  • Team size: 4 members

I worked with three other students throughout this project. I contributed largely to user research: developing guiding questions to lead our process, conducting competitor reviews and interviews, and aiding in the analysis of current dealerships' service blueprints.

Research

Understanding current dealership approach

We began with guiding questions to ground our understanding of inter-dealership communication and focus our research efforts. These include:

  1. Why do dealerships need/want to talk to each other?
  2. Why would they partner with other dealerships?
  3. How does this impact the overall customer experience?
  4. How do dealerships currently communicate?

With these questions in mind, we launched into a comparative analysis to identify how other organizations maintained consistent customer experiences across different branches. Platforms included the Health Information Exchange (HIE), Multiple Listing Service (MLS), Nitto Tire Dealer, Honda Information Network. Dealer Daily, and Polaris Ride Ready.

Made with HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap.
